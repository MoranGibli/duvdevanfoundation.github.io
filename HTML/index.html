About the unit

Duvdevan Unit

The Duvdevan Unit was established in June 1986, with the understanding that a specific and intelligent warfare method needed to be developed to deal with Judea and Samaria's security incidents, where there is a dense civilian population. The Duvdevan Unit is an elite unit, professional in its field, and dedicated to fighting in various field descriptors. The Unit works to prevent hostile terrorist activity and operates openly and disguised among a local Arab population.

The Unit’s soldiers are engaged in ongoing operational activities, carry out nocturnal arrests, seize perpetrators, create ambushes, and many undercover operations. Over the years, The Duvdevan Unit has been recognized for its intensive and important work and has received numerous accolades and medals. Shaul Mofaz, 2001

In 2001, the Unit was awarded the Prime Minister's Award for Special Activity by Prime Minister Ariel Sharon, which was, in fact, the first time that a military unit won such an award. Also, the unit holds three citations from the Chiff Of Staff

During the rise in terrorism that began in 2015, the Unit’s activity was important and necessary in the daily struggle against terror. Most of the significant arrests during this period are credited to the Unit. As a result of the intense and important activity in this wave of terror, the IDF Chief of Staff awarded the unit a heroic medal

About the Foundation

The Duvdevan Foundation was established in 1992 by a bereaved father who lost his son in the Duvdevan Unit. This Foundation has been recognized as a legal charity by federal law and is registered as “Friends of Duvdevan" (Tax ID – 83-0860160
